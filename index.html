The idea

Our idea to launch the BUN project originated from my first visit to Africa. In Liberia, a country with the flag and constitution similar to that of the United States, people here live a totally different life. I was overwhelmed by the misery that so many people in hunger could even not afford normal food like sandwiches and burgers. Leaving the capital Monrovia for local villages, our car could no longer move on the muddy roads so I kept walking until local residents came into my sight. It shocked me that a family gave birth to seven children, which all looked emaciated and suffered from malnutrition. I tried to help them but there was not any store or groceries around. All I had in my backpack was some buns left over from breakfast. I soon gave away all the buns to the children and they immediately ate them up. I could see from their eyes and faces that they had never tasted that and having enough to eat was some blessings for them. Buns as well reminded me of Easter hot cross buns, which symbolize resurrection. Indeed, simplest food like buns can bring new life to those hungry children who would otherwise be starving. That is why we name our project as BUN and plan to buy food with the money raised from the project to save more hungry children.
